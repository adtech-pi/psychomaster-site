Before You Begin

This quiz is a self-reflection tool, not couples therapy. It is designed to help you understand your instinctive patterns in conflict — not to diagnose, pathologize, or replace professional support.

If you are in a relationship that feels unsafe, if conflict is causing you real harm, or if you find yourself constantly walking on eggshells — please reach out to a licensed therapist or counselor. You deserve professional, personalized support.

Take this quiz because you're curious. Stay because you want to understand yourself better. That's enough.

No personal data is stored. Your answers stay private.

How Do You Fight in Relationships?

The way you argue tells you everything about how you love.

Most fights aren't about the thing you think they're about.

That argument about the dishes? It's not really about the dishes.

The fight about whose family to spend holidays with? It's not really about holidays.

The silent treatment that lasted three days? It's not really about whatever started it.

The way you fight — your instinctive, automatic response when things go wrong — is one of the most revealing patterns in your relationships. It comes from somewhere deep: your attachment style, your nervous system, the family you grew up in, the wounding you don't talk about.

This quiz reveals your Conflict Attachment Pattern — the specific way you show up when connection is threatened or conflict erupts.

It's not about fixing you. It's about helping you see the pattern you've been running on autopilot — so you can finally choose something different.

Be honest with yourself. There are no correct answers.
